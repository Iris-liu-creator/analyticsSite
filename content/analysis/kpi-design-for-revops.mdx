## Why KPI design matters

RevOps dashboards can become noisy when every team adds metrics without a shared decision model. Good KPI design starts with the operating question: what decision should this metric improve?

## A useful structure

Group metrics by leading indicators, conversion points, risk indicators, and business outcomes. This creates a clear distinction between what happened and what teams can still influence.

## Common mistakes

- Reporting activity without tying it to progress
- Mixing definitions across teams
- Showing averages without segment context
- Tracking too many metrics without ownership

## Practical recommendation

For each KPI, document the owner, source system, refresh cadence, formula, and expected action. If no action exists, the metric may be descriptive rather than operational.
