## Executive summary

This project demonstrates a review-first AI analytics workflow where data refreshes trigger draft commentary, anomaly prompts, and stakeholder-ready summaries.

## Business context

Recurring performance updates often require analysts to rewrite similar commentary each week. The opportunity was to automate first drafts while keeping human review and metric context in control.

## Problem statement

Manual reporting commentary consumed time that could be spent investigating causes, improving data quality, and advising stakeholders.

## Data sources

- Weekly KPI snapshots
- Dashboard metric exports
- Segment and channel performance tables
- Commentary history and business definitions

## Tools and methods

The workflow uses SQL and Python to prepare metric deltas, Databricks for scalable processing patterns, and Power BI for final dashboard context.

## Results and business impact

The workflow reduced drafting effort and created a more consistent review process for weekly insights. It also made anomaly detection more explicit.

## Lessons learned

AI is most useful in BI when it is grounded in trusted metrics, transparent assumptions, and a human approval loop.
