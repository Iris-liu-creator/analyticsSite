## Executive summary

This project explored support demand patterns and translated ticket-level activity into operational insights for managers and service leaders.

## Business context

Support leaders needed to understand why backlog was growing, which customers were most affected, and where process improvements would create the most leverage.

## Problem statement

Ticket queues were monitored with volume counts, but the team lacked a clear view of effort, complexity, repeat issues, and aging patterns.

## Data sources

- Support ticket events
- Customer segment data
- Product area classifications
- SLA and escalation records

## Tools and methods

SQL was used for event modeling, Python for exploratory analysis, and Power BI for the operating dashboard. The analysis separated simple volume from operational burden.

## Results and business impact

The work helped managers identify repeat issue clusters, improve backlog review rituals, and prioritize enablement for high-friction product areas.

## Lessons learned

Support analytics needs both customer empathy and operational rigor. A clean queue metric is useful, but root cause visibility changes behavior.
