## Executive summary

This case study shows how a RevOps reporting experience can bring sales, marketing, and finance into one shared view of pipeline health. The dashboard is designed around weekly operating rhythms rather than a static collection of charts.

## Business context

Leadership needed a single source of truth for pipeline generation, stage conversion, forecast coverage, and bookings risk. Existing reports were split across spreadsheets, CRM exports, and ad hoc dashboard pages.

## Problem statement

The reporting process was too manual, metric definitions were inconsistent, and teams had different interpretations of pipeline quality. The goal was to standardize definitions and reduce preparation time for forecast reviews.

## Data sources

- CRM opportunity and account tables
- Marketing campaign attribution exports
- Finance bookings and revenue snapshots
- Territory and segment mapping tables

## Tools and methods

Power BI provided the semantic layer and dashboard experience. SQL models prepared the source data, while DAX measures handled forecast coverage, conversion, attainment, and aging logic.

## Process and methodology

I started with stakeholder interviews, mapped the operating questions, and grouped metrics into executive, manager, and analyst views. The final design emphasized exception handling: which segments are behind, where conversion is falling, and which deals are at risk.

## Results and business impact

The dashboard gave teams a shared operating view and reduced manual weekly reporting preparation. It also improved conversations around pipeline quality by exposing coverage and conversion metrics in one place.

## Lessons learned

The most valuable dashboards are not the densest dashboards. They are the ones aligned to recurring decisions, shared definitions, and clear ownership.
